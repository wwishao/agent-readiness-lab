## Reddit / Community Educational Post

Title: A simple way to choose your first AI agent workflow without wasting weeks

Body:

I keep seeing small teams ask which AI agent workflow they should build first.

The pattern I’ve noticed: the tool matters less than the workflow choice.

A good first pilot usually has:

- high repetition
- predictable inputs
- clear output quality
- low-to-medium risk
- fast human review
- reversible mistakes
- measurable business value

I use a 7-factor scorecard for this. Score each from 1-5. Anything under roughly 24/35 probably should not be the first pilot.

I would also avoid full autonomy at the start. Most SMB pilots should begin as Observe, Advise, or Act with approval.

I turned this into a small audit kit, but the core idea above is enough to avoid the common mistake: automating a messy or risky workflow first.

Happy to share the checklist if useful.
